Basic Components

Resistor

A resistor is a passive two-terminal electrical component that implements electrical resistance as a circuit element. Resistors act to reduce current flow, and, at the same time, act to lower voltage levels within circuits.

Capacitor

A capacitor is a passive two-terminal electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field. It is typically used to store charge temporarily, to power the load when required, and to smooth out voltage fluctuations in power supplies.

LED

An LED (light-emitting diode) is a semiconductor light source that emits light when current flows through it. LEDs are used in various applications, including indicators, lighting, and displays.

Inductor

An inductor, also called a coil or reactor, is a passive two-terminal electrical component that stores energy in a magnetic field when electric current flows through it.

Diode

A diode is a two-terminal electronic component that allows current to flow in one direction only. It acts as a one-way valve for electrical current and is commonly used for rectification, voltage regulation, and signal demodulation.

Transistor

A transistor is a semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals and electrical power. It can be configured in various ways to perform functions such as amplification, switching, voltage regulation, and signal modulation.

Potentiometer

A potentiometer, also known as a variable resistor or pot, is a three-terminal resistor with a sliding or rotating contact that allows adjustment of resistance. It is used for volume control, voltage regulation, and tuning in electronic circuits.

Transformer

A transformer is an electrical device that transfers electrical energy between two or more circuits through electromagnetic induction. It is used to step up or step down voltage levels, isolate circuits, and match impedance between components.

Symbols

In electronics, various symbols are used to represent components and circuits in schematic diagrams. These symbols help engineers and technicians understand and analyze electronic circuits.
